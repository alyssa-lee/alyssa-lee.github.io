![The design of everyday things (book cover)](../../../assets/img/the-design-of-everyday-things.jpg){: style="float: right; margin: 5px 20px; max-width: 200px"}

Wow, super interesting book about how many everyday objects have design flaws that make them unnecessarily hard to use. Notable quote: "Somehow, when a device as simple as a door has to come with an instruction manual-- even a one-word manual-- then it is a failure, poorly designed."

The author also emphasizes several principles of *good* design that will help one avoid the worst pitfalls. In the case of the door, if the door has a flat metal panel on one side, you will intuitively know to push; if the door has no handle at all, you will be utterly confused.

Very relevant to an aspiring software engineer like me, if only as a reminder that we have UI/UX people for good reason. Especially relevant for people designing important things like control panels in airplanes and nuclear power plants. This book will definitely change the way you think about the minor frustrations you encounter on a daily basis.
